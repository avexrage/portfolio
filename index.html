01

The Landing Page

Visual Hierarchy & Value

Prioritized the headline "Fresh Yogurt Made From Real Fruit" over the brand name to reduce cognitive load. If users understand the value instantly, they are more likely to convert.

Brand Consistency

The "Pre-Order Now" button utilizes the primary brand blue to create a strong CTA aligning with the logo identity.

Differentiation

Placed "100% Natural & Homemade" as a pre-attentive attribute above the headline, signaling uniqueness before the user even reads the main text.

02

Transparency & Trust

Data Visualization

Replaced dense tables with horizontal progress bars. This leverages pre-attentive processing, allowing users to grasp nutritional density (e.g., High Calcium) instantly.

Function-Oriented UX

Introduced functional tags like "Bone Health" and "Immunity Boost", translating Flavor into Benefit to help users decide based on health goals.

External Validation

Cited specific lab verification (Chem-Mix Pratama) to transform the "homemade" perception from a potential risk into a verified strength.

03

Product Display

Information Architecture

Organized catalog by consumption method (Drink, Scoop, Ice Lolly) rather than flavor. This matches the user's mental model of form-factor first.

Contextual Photography

Used environmental photography (bottles in fridges, cups on ice) to visually communicate freshness and temperature, creating a sensory connection.

Interaction Design

"See More" utilizes progressive disclosure, reducing main page clutter while inviting deeper engagement with product lines.

04

Narrative Strategy

Brand Humanization

Explicitly mentioned the brand's origins as a high school project. This frames the product as a passion project, deepening the emotional connection.

Bridging Online & Offline

Utilized actual photographs of partner storefronts (Djawa Snack) rather than generic icons. This acts as social proof and aids physical wayfinding.

Distance Awareness

Designed a prominent "Can't find us?" card. This interactive component ensures geographic distance doesn't result in missed opportunities by directing users to pre-order online.

05

Conversion Destination

Filtering & Findability

Implemented a sticky tab-filter mechanism (Drink, Scoop, Ice Lolly) allowing users to switch instantly, reducing friction for repeat buyers.

Managing Expectations

Used specific microcopy "Add to Pre-order" instead of generic "Buy". This transparently communicates lead times, crucial for a homemade business model.

Visual Clarity

Card design places price and product name at the same hierarchy level to facilitate quick comparison scanning without requiring a click-through.
